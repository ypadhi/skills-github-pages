# PART I — PYQ ANALYSIS SUMMARY (What history tells you)

## A · UPSC CSE Prelims 2021-2025 — subject weightage

| Subject | 5-yr Avg | Trend |
|---|---|---|
| Polity & Governance | ~14 | Stable pillar |
| Economy | ~15 | **Rising** (21 Qs in 2025!) |
| Environment & Ecology | ~15 | Stable high (highest-ROI subject) |
| Geography | ~13 | Stable; spike in 2024 (18 Qs) |
| History (total) | ~14 | Modern 7, Ancient 4, Med 2, Art&Culture 3 |
| Science & Tech | ~12 | Current-affairs flavoured now |
| International Relations | ~7 | **Rising** (geopolitics anchored) |
| Schemes (standalone) | ~4 | Falling (now mixed into other subjects) |

**Big shifts to know:**
- CSAT became a real filter in 2023 (cutoff crashed to 75.41 — all-time low). Plan it as a paper, not a formality.
- Medieval History collapsed (1 Q is common now); Art & Culture also slipping.
- IR and Economy both rising.
- 2025 paper saw a direct PYQ repeat (SAHEL question from 2023) — PYQs predict.
- Statement-based and Assertion-Reason MCQs dominate; pure factual recall is fading.

## B · OPSC OCS Prelims — the 2024 game-changer

**The biggest single insight in this whole research:** OCS Paper I went from ~4 Odisha-specific questions (2021-2023) to **~20 Odisha-specific questions in 2024** — roughly 20% of the paper. Plan as if 2026 will be the same or higher.

| Subject | 2024 share (out of 100) |
|---|---|
| **Polity & Governance** | ~14 |
| **Indian History** | ~10 |
| **Odisha History & Culture** | **6-7** ⬆ |
| **Indian Geography** | ~6 |
| **Odisha Geography** | **4-5** ⬆ |
| **Indian Economy** | ~6 |
| **Odisha Economy / Schemes** | **3-4** ⬆ |
| **Environment & Ecology** | ~10 |
| **Science & Tech** | ~9 |
| **Current Affairs (national + Odisha)** | ~11 |
| **Sports / misc** | ~3 |
| **Total Odisha-tilted** | **~19-20** |

- OCS is **more factual** and less analytical than UPSC. Match-the-following, single-fact MCQs dominate.
- Cutoff under new 100-Q format: ~72-80 marks UR / 200 (i.e., ~50-55 right answers with minimal wrong).
- CSAT in OCS = 33% qualifying. Don't underestimate.

## Mock Test Plan (non-negotiable)

You should attempt **at least 4 full-length Paper I mocks + 2 CSAT mocks** in the next 12 days:
- **Testbook OPSC OAS Test Series** (cheapest, large bank)
- **APTI PLUS OPSC Test Series** (most exam-aligned)
- **EduRev OPSC OAS 2026** (free supplemental)
- Free: **Adda247 Odisha**, **SRIAS open mocks** if available

After every mock: **2-hour analysis cycle.** Read every option of every question (even the ones you got right). Note errors in a single notebook — review it before exam.

## Exam day rules

1. **Aim 60-65 attempts** in Paper I (out of 100). Each wrong = -0.66.
2. Walk away from any question you can't reduce to 2 options.
3. CSAT — solve in order: Verbal RC → Reasoning → Quant. Aim 30+ attempts for 90+ marks.
4. OMR — fill bubbles as you go (don't save till the end).
5. Hydrate, light meal. No new revision in the last 2 hours before exam.

# PART VI — KEY DISCIPLINE RULES

1. **One source per subject.** Never read two Polity books simultaneously.
2. **Don't subscribe to >5 Telegram channels.** Noise kills signal.
3. **PYQs are predictive.** Solve UPSC 2013-2025 + OCS last 10 years — twice each.
4. **CSAT is no longer optional.** 30% of your prep bandwidth in the final 4 months.
5. **Statement-based MCQs** — practice elimination, not memorization.
6. **Revision > new content.** A book read 3 times beats 3 books read once.
7. **For OCS specifically — Odisha is 20%.** Bookmark APTI PLUS / Drishti Odisha as daily ritual.
8. **Mock analysis is more important than mock attempt.** 2 hrs analysis per mock minimum.
9. **Newspaper habit from Day 1.** The Hindu, 45 minutes. Editorial + Explained + National.
10. **Track your weak-areas notebook.** It becomes your final-week revision document.

# PART VII — IF YOU'RE STUCK / CONFUSED

These are the situations and the right action:

| Situation | Do this |
|---|---|
| "I don't have a Prelims test series subscription" | Forum IAS 2026 (~₹10k) is the best value pick. Or use free: Drishti Pre-Prayaas + Insights STEP UP + Vision Open Mock |
| "I haven't read NCERTs in years" | Skip them now. Use Vision PT365 + your existing notes. Add NCERTs in Phase 1 of CSE 2027 prep |
| "I'm scoring below 60 in mocks" | Cut mock attempts, increase revision. Mock score below cutoff in the last 12 days = revision deficit |
| "CSAT scares me" | Take 1 mock immediately. If you scored above 80, just maintain. If below 66, this is your biggest risk — make it priority 1 |
| "Too much current affairs to revise" | PT365 is the consolidated version. If still overwhelmed, just use Vision Monthly (last 6 months) + UPDATES_2024_to_2026.md |
| "I keep forgetting facts" | Make 1-page flashcards (handwritten). Use Anki app if digital-friendly. Revise the same flashcard set 5-7 times before exam |
